Bonsai Simulator

2024
UnityC#Blender
Submitted for ProcJam 2024, Bonsai Simulator is an artistic representation of tree growth. Care for a potted sapling using simplified bonsai care methods. Trim, prune and wire a potted tree as it ages.
Timelapse of bonsais generated at different growth stages. Each bonsai has slightly different growth patterns which affect branch strength and trunk expansion rate. Branch growth is propogated in the form of energy, starting at the base of the tree and working its way upwards. The development of a branch is limited so that the sum of the girth of the branches does not exceed the girth of the trunk.

About

Bonsai Simulator is all about representing the real processes of tree growth. Care for a potted sapling using simplified bonsai care methods. Trim, prune and wire a potted tree as it ages.

My goal was to create a system that represented trees as ongoing developing organisms, not just a fixed structure at a single point in time. Rather than having complete control over the growth of the bonsai, the tree grows according to an annual system and as a player you have limited tools to influence the tree's appearance. What you choose to prune will affect not just the appearance of the bonsai, but also it's health in the future.

Development Process

This project was heavily inspired by actual bonsai care practices. I researched the growth patterns of trees both prior to and throughout development. This research not only added realism to my simulation but also simplified the design of the system by keeping only systems based on real natural processes.

My system's design revolves around three main programming concepts: interfaces, vectors, and of course, trees.

  • Trees (data structure):

    The benefit of using this data structure is that there is already lots of literature and researched systems that can be applied to make my life easier when modeling an actual tree (recursion, tree traversals, branching).

  • Interfaces

    To coordinate growth across the tree, I implement an interface called "Growth." Each segment of the tree may grow in a different way or perform different actions at the end of every year, but the interface allows these different actions to be coordinated and invoked at once.

  • Vectors

    Each branch is represented by a sum of vectors which determines it's direction and length. In addition to a branch's facing direction, a weight vector is computed based on the length and girth of all branches above a given branch.

Attributions

My main references were articles on The Grove 3D (to understand tree growth patterns,) as well as two books, Principles of Bonsai Design by David De Groot, and The Bonsai Beginner's Bible by Peter Chan, (to inform my decisions when rendering the tree's appearance.)

The Desert

2024
UnityC#GDAL
An augmented reality, geospatial data experiment. Look through your phone camera and see a digital mirror of our world, reconstructed from your GPS position and local weather data.
A test of the lighting system over Mount Rainier, Washington. Geography is automatically generated from longitude and latitude coordinates.
Read the devlog View the final game pitch

Engine: Unity

Platforms: iOS, Mobile Web

Duration: In Progress

Team Size: Me

About

The Desert is a calm, meditative, mobile experience that makes your phone into a window through which one sees the world transformed into a barren sandy landscape. The Desert was first pitched as part of a game narratives course. The game is about helping people pay more attention to the experiences we have interacting with our physical environments.

Development Process

I am working on this game as a solo project. So far, I have written custom APIs to process geospatial data in Unity and handle downloading terrain data based on player location. I have also designed in engine terrain lighting and coded a system for rendering time of day according to the time of day in the real world.

Attributions

This project makes partial use of code from the Virtual Reality Geospatial Information System (ViRGIS) project to process geographical data from the United States Geological Survey (USGS).

Lunar Lander Reinforcement Learning Agent

2024
Java
A recreation of the arcade title, Lunar Lander, plus an AI controlled player which uses reinforcement learning. Both are programmed entirely from scratch.
The final iteration of the AI agent was trained for one hour of game time. It still makes mistakes, but lands successfully more than 80% of the time, and on score zones around 60% of the time.
Download the game Read the code Read the report

Engine: Custom (Swing for rendering)

Platforms: Java VM

Duration: One Month

Team Size: Me

About

Lunar Lander is an arcade game about landing a spacecraft on the moon with a limited amount of fuel. This project combines various reinforcement learning techniques to train an AI to land the spaceship correctly while using as little fuel as possible.

Development Process

The first stage of the project involved recreating Lunar Lander in Java. I wrote custom physics and rendered the game using only shapes and drawing techniques native to the Java Swing package. For the AI portion of the project, I designed a separate engine for computing the physics and information necessary for the AI to play the game. Then I modified the original game to allow the AI agent to play, including features that would make training and modifying the AI easier.

Engine: Unity

About

The Park Behind my House is an explorable, hand-painted and hand-modeled 3D environment. It features a dynamic music system and lots of environmental animations, from moving clouds to swaying leaves, blowing grass, sunrays and mist.

Development Process

As a submission to a traditional art show, the focus of this project was it's environment and the acryllic painting that makes up most of the game's textures. The goal of this project was to work on every part of the game development pipeline. This is best seen in the game's playable character, which I drew concept art for, then modeled, textured, and animated in Blender. I then handled movement controls, footstep sounds, and controlling the animations in Unity. The player model is shaded with a custom toon shader, and natural swaying effects are all rendered with HLSL.

Attributions

All music was composed and performed by a classmate, Xander. This project makes use of some Unity store nature assets for its forest zone and some Quixel textures for the ground in the maze zone.
